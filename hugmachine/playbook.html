Hug Machine Playbook

Offense

We have a variety of initiation plays to get the disc moving. After the first few moves, we will move into flow with the goal of keeping the disc moving quickly.

River

Horizontal intiation that focuses on a wide central cutting lane.

  • Cutters align in pairs, and take turns cutting
  • Cutters clear to the same side they started from
  • Clearing moves are viable cuts - if the defender stays in the lane, the handlers should hit the clearing player
  • Deep strikes are available down the sidelines after clearing

Windmill

Horizontal initiation to create space on the open side.

  • The initial break movement from the cutters is open for a quick throw, these should be proper cuts

Shitbox

A modified vert look, isolating the first two cutters to create space.

  • Think of the Shitbox like an endzone play, the iso cutter gets several chances to cut
  • Handlers should move the disc as necessary to open up space for the iso

Empire

Vertical stack initiation

  • Cut deep from the front of the stack
  • The back two players of the stack should try to occupy their defenders
  • The back of the stack is the second cut for all the variations

Endzone

We set up our endzone with a four player stack. The front player is isolated with lots of space to cut. The front player should set up a few yards to the open side to provide multiple options for the handler.

Endzone Iso

The primary cutter is isolated at the front of the stack. The iso sets up a few yards to the open side to provide multiple options to the handler.

  • There are three spaces for the iso to attack: open side, inside break, and around break
  • Setting up on the open side means the inside break is an easier shallow break

Endzone Open Side

Endzone sideline play.

  • The front of the stack is the primary bail cut
  • The continuation cut is from the back of the stack

Endzone Break Side

Endzone sideline play.

  • The front of the stack is the primary bail cut
  • The continuation cut is from the back of the stack

Defense

Our defensive strategies are based around team defense. Even when playing person-to-person, we'll look to take advantage of switches to disrupt the offense and keep matchups in our favour. Our zone looks aim to generate turnovers by putting pressure on the throwers and taking away their simplest options.

Monster

Zone look requiring a strong mark.

  • The mark forces straight up to prevent throws directly downfield
  • The ears take away the handler's throwing lanes and upfield swings
  • Wings adjust based on the ear positioning, and take away continuation cuts after swings
  • The mid-mid covers the gaps between the monster and ears, and helps the wings on swings
  • The mark will shade their force towards the closer sideline as the disc moves

Hug Zone

Aggressive zone with a strong sideline trap.

  • The trap call is 'HUG!'
  • The mark forces to the close sideline
  • The wall closes on crashers, and takes away downfield throws
  • The wings and deep patrol the areas behind the wall
  • On a trap, the mark forces no dump, and the wall forms a box around the thrower, cutting off the swings

Hug Zone vs Swings

A further look at the motion of the hugging zone

  • The mark forces sideline, and on disc movement generally forces to the same direction
  • Trap both sidelines

Hug Zone with Switches

The upgraded version of the hug zone has the mids switch into the trap

  • On a throw to the sideline, the close side wing closes off downfield throws, and takes the role of the top corner of the trap
  • The furthest player in the wall drops back to cover cross field throws
  • The farther wing moves across the field to cover the space behind the trap
  • If the disc is centered, the original wall re-forms

Switches

In general, be aware of your teammates! If you can cover a dangerous cut or space, do so and let your teammates know. If you see a teammate in a good position to cover a cut, let them know!

  • One helpful switch is to drop off a swing handler to cover a give-and-go
